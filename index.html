Peanut-sweet potato croquettes

This is a recipe for peanut-sweet potato croquettes, adapted from George Washington Carver's Recipe No. 38 here. Instead of spreading a peanut-based slurry on solid sweet potato slices, I puree the ingredients together into a single croquette. I suggest frying the ingredients in ghee instead of oil, though if you chose oil I suggest peanut oil or sunflower oil. I also replace salt and pepper with seaweed-based ingredients, bread crumbs with panko, the latter of which was partially replaced in turn by peanut flour

Ingredients

  • 150 g roasted, unsalted peanuts
  • 1 sweet potato, chopped and boiled
  • 10 g kelp powder
  • Pinch dulse granules (salt substitute)
  • 1 egg
  • 40 g panko
  • 25 g peanut flour
  • Ghee for frying

For the seaweed ingredients, I get mine from Maine Coast Sea Vegetables For the peanut flour, it is a byproduct of this peanut milk recipe from the National Peanut Board

Directions

  1. Gather dry ingredients and place them in a food processor. Put thin layer of paper towels on a large plate for later.
  2. Blanch peanuts until oily, then drain, about five minutes. Meanwhile, boil sweet potatoes until soft, about 10 minutes, then drain.
  3. Put cooked ingredients in food processor. Also crack an egg and put it too in the food processor. Meanwhile, heat up ghee in pan on high.
  4. Turn on food processor and blend until largely smooth and moderately sticky.
  5. Form mixture into patties and fry in hot ghee until golden brown and firm.
  6. Remove from heat and sop up excess oiliness by sitting on paper towels.
  7. Serve hot.
